## 📌 REGULATORY BASELINE (Current as of April 22, 2026)

### Master Circulars & Key Regulations

**1. SEBI Master Circular - January 30, 2026**
- **Reference:** SEBI/HO/CFD/PoD-2/CIR/P/0155
- **Scope:** Consolidates all operative instructions under LODR Regulations 2015
- **Coverage:** Through December 30, 2025
- **Status:** SUPERSEDES prior Master Circulars (Nov 2024, July 2023)
- **Rescissions:** 50+ earlier circulars rescinded to eliminate redundancy
- **Key Focus:** Streamlined compliance, enhanced governance, investor protection

**2. SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) (Fifth Amendment) Regulations, 2025**
- **Reference:** SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2025/273
- **Published:** November 18, 2025
- **Effective:** November 19, 2025
- **Major Changes:**
  - Related Party Transaction (RPT) thresholds realigned with Schedule XII (turnover-based materiality framework)
  - Revised definitions: Directors, KMP, and relatives
  - Removal of earlier provisos under Regulation 12
  - Stricter Audit Committee approvals for high-value subsidiary transactions (lacking 1-year audited financials)

**3. SEBI (LODR) (Sixth Amendment) Regulations, 2025**
- **Reference:** SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2025/289
- **Published:** December 16, 2025
- **Major Changes:**
  - Restructuring of High Value Debt Listed Entity (HVDLE) regime
  - Direct credit of securities in dematerialized form (Regulation 39 substituted)
  - Unified framework for transfer of unclaimed amounts to IEPF (Regulation 61A)

**4. SEBI (LODR) (Amendment) Regulations, 2026**
- **Reference:** SEBI/NRO-GN/2026/295
- **Published:** January 20, 2026
- **Effective:** Immediate
- **Changes:** Continuation of governance strengthening initiatives

## 🎯 CRITICAL DISCLOSURE TIMELINES: REGULATION 30

### Industry Standards Note (February 25, 2025)
**Reference:** SEBI/HO/CFD/CFD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2025/25

Issued in consultation with Industry Standards Forum to bring uniformity in materiality determination and disclosure practices.

### Four-Tier Timeline Framework:

#### **TIER 1: 30 MINUTES** ⚡
**Trigger:** Board meeting closure where decision on material event is taken
**Material Sequence:** 
- Board meeting → Decision taken → Closure of meeting → 30 min clock starts
**Examples:**
- Acquisition/Merger approval
- Major contract award/termination
- Merger announcement
- Change in business model
**Applicable Category:** Para A items of Schedule III requiring Board decision

**Compliance Notes:**
- Timing starts from actual closure (not commencement) of Board meeting
- If Board decision extends over multiple days, clock starts from final closure
- Filed directly to stock exchange(s) within timeline

---

#### **TIER 2: 12 HOURS** ⏱️
**Trigger:** Event emanating from within the listed entity
**Material Sequence:**
- Event occurs within company operations → 12-hour clock starts
**Examples:**
- Fraud/irregularities discovered
- Major regulatory action/show cause notice
- Facility damage/accident
- Key personnel resignation/death
- Material litigation filed
- Regulatory penalty/order
**Applicable Category:** Para B events and internal material information

**Compliance Notes:**
- "Occurrence" defined per SEBI circular Sept 9, 2015 (Annexure II)
- Company determines when an officer becomes aware through credible and verifiable channels
- Companies must document their internal SOPs defining these channels

---

#### **TIER 3: 24 HOURS** ⏳
**Trigger:** Material event from external sources
**Material Sequence:**
- External event affects company → 24-hour clock starts
**Examples:**
- Major customer loss (>5% revenue)
- Supplier default/bankruptcy
- Competitor action affecting business
- External regulatory order
- Market condition materially affecting business
**Applicable Category:** External material information

**Compliance Notes:**
- Must be reported by external party or become known to company officer through credible channels
- Company assessment of materiality is critical

---

#### **TIER 4: 2 WORKING DAYS** 📅
**Trigger:** AGM/EGM proceedings outcomes
**Material Sequence:**
- Meeting → Proceedings occur → 2 working days for complete results disclosure
**Regulatory Requirements:**
- Within 12 hours: Basic meeting details (Regulation 30(6)(ii))
- Within 2 working days: Complete voting results (Regulation 44(3))
**Applicable Category:** Para A(13) Schedule III - Board/AGM/EGM proceedings

**Compliance Notes:**
- Separate timelines: First announcement (12 hours), then complete results (2 days)
- Counted from next working day if meeting on Saturday/evening

---

### Materiality Assessment Framework

**Quantitative Thresholds (Company-Specific):**

1. **Revenue Impact Test**
   - Threshold: Company defines (typically 5-10% of annual turnover)
   - Example: ₹100 Cr annual revenue → ₹5-10 Cr transaction = material

2. **EBITDA Impact Test**
   - Threshold: 3-5% EBITDA impact
   - Trending/forward impact also considered

3. **Balance Sheet Impact Test**
   - Asset impact: >10% of total assets
   - Liability impact: >10% of total liabilities

4. **Qualitative Factors:**
   - Strategic importance to business
   - Impact on corporate structure
   - Regulatory/compliance consequences
   - Market perception/reaction

**Special Cases (Per Industry Standards Note):**
- Board resignations: Material per se (disclose within 12 hours)
- Major litigation: Disclose if >₹10 Lakh penalty or material quantum
- Regulatory penalties: >₹5 Lakh from regulatory body = material
- Fraud detection: Always material (immediate disclosure)

---

### Defense for Delayed Disclosure

Per Industry Standards Note, reasonable defenses for delayed filing:
✓ Time needed for prima facie assessment of materiality
✓ Awaiting regulatory approval/clearance
✓ Confidentiality agreements/legal constraints
✓ Technical/system issues (with evidence)
✓ Awaiting 3rd party confirmation

**Requirement:** If delayed beyond timeline, MUST file with detailed explanation for delay.

## 📋 RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS: FIFTH AMENDMENT FRAMEWORK

### New RPT Materiality Thresholds (Per Schedule XII)

**Turnover-Based Framework (Effective Nov 2025):**

| Transaction Value | % of Annual Turnover | Approval Level | Key Changes (Fifth Amendment) |
|------------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| Upto 10% | < 10% turnover | Board approval | Standard threshold |
| 10-25% | 10-25% turnover | Audit Committee + Board | Enhanced scrutiny |
| Above 25% | > 25% turnover | Shareholder approval | Regulation 23 applies |
| **Subsidiary Transactions** | **Any value >1 Cr** | **Enhanced AC review** | **NEW: High-value subsidiary transactions require detailed AC review if subsidiary has no 1-year audited financials** |

### Definition Changes (Fifth Amendment):

**Directors:** Expanded to include nominees and indirect interests
**KMP:** Enhanced to cover Chief Finance Officer, Company Secretary positions
**Relatives:** Now includes spouse, parent, adult sibling, adult child relationships
**Related Parties:** Any entity with 20%+ voting rights or control

### Audit Committee Responsibilities (Enhanced):

1. Prior review of all RPT proposals
2. Assessment of fairness & arm's length nature
3. Determination of materiality per Schedule XII
4. For high-value subsidiary deals: Enhanced financial review
5. Minutes documentation with full disclosures
6. Quarterly reporting to Board

## 🔄 INTEGRATED FILING SYSTEM (Effective April 1, 2025)

### NSE/BSE Implementation

**Change:** PDF submission for financial results DISCONTINUED
**New Requirement:** XBRL-based Integrated Filing System mandatory

**Components:**

1. **Integrated Filing - Financial**
   - XBRL submission mandatory
   - Replaces separate utilities for:
     - Financial results
     - Related party transactions
     - Audit qualifications
     - Deviation statements
   - PDF: Submit same day as XBRL

2. **Integrated Filing - Governance**
   - XBRL-based disclosure
   - Board composition
   - Related party transactions detail
   - Corporate governance matrix

3. **Implementation Timeline:**
   - Phase 1 (April 1, 2025): Financial results
   - Phase 2 (Ongoing): Governance filings
   - PDFs phased out progressively

**Compliance Checklist:**
- ☑ PDF results submitted within 30 mins / 3 hours
- ☑ XBRL Integrated Filing on same day
- ☑ Related party transaction details in XBRL
- ☑ Deviation statement in XBRL
- ☑ No separate utilities needed
- ☑ API-based integration available for frequent filers

## 📊 BRSR CORE ESG REPORTING

### Regulatory Framework & Applicability

**Effective:** FY 2023-24 onwards (Mandatory for top 1000 companies)
**Updated:** SEBI BRSR Core (July 2023)
**Latest Guidance:** BRSR Industry Standards (2024-25)

### Phased Applicability (By Market Cap):

**FY 2023-24:**
- Top 150 companies: BRSR Core voluntary disclosures

**FY 2024-25:**
- Top 150 companies: BRSR Core with 3rd-party assessment (mandatory)
- Top 151-500: BRSR Core (voluntary, assessment optional)

**FY 2025-26:**
- Top 150 companies: BRSR Core + assurance mandatory
- Top 151-500: BRSR Core with assessment mandatory
- Value chain ESG: Voluntary for top 250 (phased glide path)

**FY 2026-27 onwards:**
- Top 500 companies: BRSR Core with assurance mandatory
- Top 501-1000: BRSR Core with assessment/assurance
- Value chain ESG: Mandatory for top 250 with assurance

### 49 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) Structure:

**A. Environmental (18 KPIs):**
1. Energy consumption (renewable/non-renewable)
2. GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3)
3. Water consumption & recycling
4. Waste management & diversion rates
5. Air emissions & pollutants
6. Product energy efficiency
7. Ecological impact mitigation
8. Supply chain environmental practices

**B. Social (18 KPIs):**
1. Gender diversity (workforce + board)
2. Employee safety (accidents, LTIFR, fatalities)
3. Occupational health & disease incidence
4. Diversity (SC/ST, OBC, PWD representation)
5. Training & skill development
6. Health insurance coverage
7. Sexual harassment complaints
8. Human rights assessment in supply chain
9. Community development programs
10. Value chain social practices

**C. Governance (13 KPIs):**
1. Board composition & independence
2. Executive remuneration & fairness
3. Business ethics & compliance
4. Whistleblower mechanism effectiveness
5. Data privacy & cybersecurity
6. Risk management systems
7. CSR spend & effectiveness
8. Supply chain governance
9. Tax transparency & compliance
10. Board diversity & expertise

### Materiality Assessment in BRSR:

Companies must identify:
- Material ESG issues per GRI Standards
- Stakeholder expectations
- Industry peer benchmarks
- Regulatory expectations
- Value chain impacts

### Assurance Requirements:

**Third-Party Assurance Providers:**
- Registered auditors
- CABs (Certification and Accreditation Bodies)
- ISO 14001/45001 certified auditors

**Assurance Levels:**
- **Limited Assurance:** Moderate confidence (most common)
- **Reasonable Assurance:** High confidence (for critical metrics)

**Record Retention:** Minimum 2 financial years for supporting documentation

## ⚖️ ENFORCEMENT & PENALTIES

### SEBI's Shift to Preventive Regulation

**Approach:** From reactive to preventive & deterrent enforcement

**Key Points:**
- Repeated minor defaults accumulate serious consequences
- Continuous disclosure failures attract significant penalties
- Non-compliance with timelines treated severely
- Record-keeping breaches subject to investigation

### Typical Penalties (Non-exhaustive):

| Violation | Typical Penalty |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Regulation 30 delay (1st instance) | ₹1-5 Lakh warning |
| Regulation 30 delay (repeated) | ₹5-50 Lakh + action |
| RPT disclosure failure | ₹10-50 Lakh |
| BRSR non-disclosure | ₹10 Lakh+ |
| Board/AGM procedural violations | ₹5-25 Lakh |
| Related party approval breach | ₹25-100 Lakh |
| Insider information mishandling | Show cause + penalties |
